My Choice for "Design Principles"

The Principles are the following:

  • PARC: Repetition
  • Visual Hierarchy
  • Fitt's Law

PARC: Repetition

Company: Microsoft

https://www.microsoft.com/

Microsoft website is one of the examples that I choose, to show the principle of repetition.

The repetion is applied throught a lot of the sections from the website, and they always use this pattern.
Image -> Title -> A brief summary -> And a link to the title section.

Visual Hierarchy

Company: The Church of Jesus Christ of the Latter Day Saints

https://myinstitute.churchofjesuschrist.org/home

In my point of view, the myinstitute website, applies the principle of Visual Hierarchy, I'll show you why.

When you get into the web page, you can instantly categorise, which parts are the most importants.
The first thing that I see is the part named "Classes", right after I see "My Classes" section, and after I see the class that I'm in and in the end I see the part saying "Classes Offered".

The Website shows a Visual Hierarchy, in my point of view.

Fitt's Law

Company: Carrefour

https://www.carrefour.com.br/informatica/notebook

Carrefour is a Brazilian company which is a Grocers store, just like Walmart.

Because Carrefour sells a big variety of products, they needed to make a website, which could facilitate the user experience.
But, what did they use to facilitate the user experience? That's right, the solution was to apply Fitt's Law as you see in the next image.

when you search for something, right at the beggining of the products list, there is a option called "Filtros" or "Filters".
If you select it, you will find a few options to use to filter your list, in this case, since I searched for a notebook, I can filter by the size of the storedge, I can filter by brand and also by a specific type of operational system.
